Nicholas Jackson

3rd Year PhD Student in Biomedical Informatics

Vanderbilt University

I believe AI has the potential to transform medicine, but progress is limited by two challenges: clinical data is often inaccessible due to privacy constraints, and models do not generalize well across populations — a disparity driven largely by data scarcity.

My research develops new methods for synthetic data generation, studies how synthetic data can improve model performance on underrepresented populations, and produces synthetic datasets that are being prepared for public release.

I am currently an intern at Oracle Labs' Machine Learning Research Group, working on statistical methods to verify the trustworthiness of AI training data. Upon returning to Vanderbilt, I will be funded by an NIH F31 fellowship to apply synthetic data generation to improve machine learning for HIV research.
